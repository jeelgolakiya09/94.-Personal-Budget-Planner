Savings is a key part of smart budgeting, supported by powerful financial theories. The Time Value of Money shows that saving early grows wealth through compound interest. Our Team Works

1. Life-Cycle Hypothesis (LCH) Theory by: Franco Modigliani and Richard Brumberg Core Idea: People aim to maintain a stable standard of living over their lifetime by saving during high-income periods (e.g., working years) and spending during low-income periods (e.g., retirement). Relation to Budgeting: Budget planners help users allocate income wisely over time, preparing for future needs (like retirement or education) while managing current expenses.

2. Permanent Income Hypothesis (PIH) Theory by: Milton Friedman Core Idea: People base consumption on their expected long-term income rather than short-term income changes. Relation to Budgeting: A personal budget should reflect not just current earnings, but future expectations (e.g., promotions, career growth). This helps avoid overspending during income spikes or stress during temporary income drops.
