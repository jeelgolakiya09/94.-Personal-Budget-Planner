Budget Components :

Income/Earnings

Income refers to the money or financial gain that an individual, business, or organization receives in exchange for providing goods, services, or through investment returns.

It can be categorized into various types such as salary, wages, business profits, rental income, dividends, and interest earnings.

Income is a key factor in determining financial stability, purchasing power, and standard of living

Savings

Savings refer to the portion of income that is not spent on current expenditures but is set aside for future use.

Individuals and households typically save money for various goals, such as building an emergency fund, purchasing a home, planning for education, or preparing for retirement.

Savings can be stored in various forms, including savings accounts, fixed deposits, or investment vehicles.

Expenses

Expenses refer to the money spent to purchase goods and services or to cover obligations.

They are a key component of personal and business financial management, representing the outflow of funds necessary for day-to-day living or operations.

Common personal expenses include housing, food, transportation, utilities, healthcare, and entertainment.
